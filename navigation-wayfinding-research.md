# Navigation/Wayfinding Domain — App Ideation Research

**Domain:** Navigation/Wayfinding
**Date:** 2026-06-16 (Session 27)
**Ecosystem:** ljding.app (learn / hangwith)

---

## Market Research Summary

### Finding 1: Navigation Apps Are Ubiquitous but Diaspora-Specific Gaps are Large

The global mapping/navigation market is mature (Google Maps, Apple Maps, Waze, Mapbox), but **English-speaking Chinese diaspora users face specific gaps**:

- **Google Maps works well in the US** but has limited coverage in China (rural areas, small businesses, Chinese-language-only venues)
- **Gaode Maps (高德地图)** and **Baidu Maps** require Chinese language proficiency
- **No web-first bilingual navigation tool exists** — all navigation apps are mobile-first
- Chinese diaspora users who travel to China regularly (shuttle families, business travelers, visiting relatives) have no good English-language wayfinding option

### Finding 2: Local Discovery for Chinese-Speaking Services in the US is Fragmented

Chinese Americans seeking Chinese-speaking doctors, lawyers, accountants, or banks outside major Chinatown areas rely on:
- WeChat group recommendations (unverified, no reviews)
- Word of mouth
- Baidu/Chinese search (requires Chinese)
- English Yelp/Google (doesn't distinguish bilingual or Chinese-speaking businesses)

**No bilingual, curated, verified directory** exists that maps Chinese-speaking service providers across US metro areas.

### Finding 3: Cultural Venue Discovery is a Genuine Gap

Chinese diaspora heritage sites, temples, community centers, Chinese museums, and cultural venues are poorly indexed on Google Maps. Most are found through WeChat groups and community networks. This creates:
- First-generation parents who want to share heritage with second-generation children but can't find venues
- Tourists/visitors who want authentic cultural experiences
- Researchers and students who need verified location data

### Finding 4: Shuttle Family Logistics are Completely Unserved

Families maintaining homes in both the US and China (shuttle families) need to navigate:
- Local services in China (contractors, repair, healthcare) with no English resources
- Local services in the US near their Chinese-speaking elderly parents
- Property management at distance (rental homes, family property)
- Transportation between two home bases

**No single tool addresses the "two-country lifestyle" navigation problem.**

### Finding 5: Web-First Pre-Trip Planning is a Gap

Most navigation apps are mobile-first, but **trip planning for visiting China happens on desktop**:
- Researching routes and destinations
- Building itineraries
- Sharing plans with family members
- Coordinating multi-person trips

Users plan on desktop, then need to access on mobile in China with poor connectivity. **No web-first bilingual China trip planning tool exists.**

## Market Gaps Summary Table

| Gap | Problem | Evidence |
|-----|---------|----------|
| **English-first China navigation** | No English-friendly web navigation for China | Google Maps limited, Gaode/Baidu require Chinese |
| **Bilingual local discovery in US** | No verified bilingual directory of Chinese-speaking businesses | WeChat groups + word of mouth only |
| **Cultural venue database** | Chinese heritage sites not on Google Maps | Found through informal networks only |
| **Shuttle family logistics** | Zero tools for dual-country lifestyle navigation | WeChat/phone is current state |
| **Web-first China trip planning** | All navigation is mobile-first | Trip planning happens on desktop, not phone |
