# Wedding & Events Planning — Research Document
> Session 35 | 2026-06-24 | Domain: Wedding & Events Planning

## Research Goals
- Identify gaps in wedding/event planning tools
- Find web-first and bilingual opportunities
- Explore diaspora-specific needs (Chinese bilingual couples)

## Competitive Landscape

### Major Players
- **The Knot** — Comprehensive wedding planning, US-centric, English only
- **Zola** — Registry + planning, US market focus
- **WeddingWire** — Vendor marketplace, directory model
- **Joy** — Modern wedding website builder, mobile-first
- **Chinese platforms**: **看海外婚礼** (Kanhaiwa), **婚礼纪** — China-focused, mobile apps, limited English

### Market Trends (2024-2026)
1. **Micro-weddings and intimate ceremonies** — Post-COVID shift to smaller, more personal events
2. **Bicultural weddings** — Increasing cross-cultural couples needing bilingual planning tools
3. **Destination weddings** — Particularly popular among diaspora communities
4. **DIY and intimate wedding fatigue** — Couples overwhelmed by Pinterest-perfect expectations
5. **Digital invitations** — Sustainability shift away from paper, accelerated by COVID
6. **Registry fragmentation** — Couples registering across multiple platforms

### Key Pain Points
1. **Guest management chaos** — Spreadsheets for tracking RSVPs, dietary restrictions, plus-ones
2. **Vendor coordination** — Email chains with multiple vendors, no central communication hub
3. **Bicultural complexity** — Navigating two family traditions, bilingual needs
4. **Budget tracking** — Surprise costs, currency conversion for international families
5. **Timeline stress** — 12-18 month planning cycles with thousands of tasks
6. **Seating arrangements** — The "seating chart problem" — NP-hard complexity
7. **Day-of coordination** — Last-minute changes, vendor morning-of communication
8. **Registry scattered** — Guests checking multiple sites, couples managing duplicates

### Web-First Opportunities
1. **No major web-first wedding planner** — Most competitors are mobile-first (The Knot app, Zola app)
2. **Desktop planners for complex events** — Destination weddings, large guest lists benefit from larger screens
3. **Collaborative planning** — Real-time collaboration for wedding party + families
4. **Bilingual (EN/ZH) gap** — Chinese-American couples have no web-native bilingual option
5. **Guest experience apps** — Web-based RSVP, dietary cards, day-of schedule

### Diaspora-Specific Gaps
1. **Currency-aware budget tracking** — RMB + USD conversions for families on both sides
2. **Bicultural ceremony planning** — Tea ceremony + Western ceremony in one timeline
3. **Bilingual invitations** — Chinese + English invitation suites
4. **Cross-border vendor coordination** — US/China vendor management
5. **Cultural tradition guidance** — 红包 (red envelope) guidance, tea ceremony protocols
6. **Multi-timezone family communication** — Extended family in China + US/Canada/UK
7. **Translation needs** — Menu cards, ceremony programs, vendor contracts

## Gap Analysis

### High Opportunity Gaps
| Gap | Opportunity | Why Now |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| Web-first bicultural wedding planner | Bilingual EN/ZH planning hub | Growing Chinese-American diaspora, no web-native solution |
| Digital RSVP + Guest management web app | No competitor is web-first | Mobile-heavy market leaves desktop gap |
| Destination wedding hub | Travel + planning integration | Growing segment, fragmented tools |
| Bilingual invitation builder | Canva lacks Chinese templates | Chinese-American couples underserved |
| Cultural tradition advisor | AI-powered tradition guidance | First AI-native wedding tool category |

### Low Opportunity Gaps
| Gap | Why Pass |
|-----|----------|
| General wedding vendor marketplace | The Knot/Zola have massive moats |
| Generic registry tool | Zola/Amazon have this locked |
| Wedding photography marketplace | Already saturated |

## Audience Segments
1. **Chinese-American bicultural couples** — Primary target, highest differentiation
2. **Diaspora couples** — Indian-American, Korean-American, other Asian-American
3. **International destination couples** — Planning from abroad for China/Asia destination
4. **Micro-wedding couples** — Intimate ceremony planners needing less complexity
5. **LGBTQ+ couples** — Often underserved by mainstream platforms

## Monetization Angles
1. **Premium tier** — Guest limit increases, advanced analytics, multi-event support
2. **Vendor featured placement** — Marketplace take-rate
3. **Print shop** — Invitation suites, programs, thank you cards
4. **Concierge planning** — Human planner matching service
5. **Gift registry take-rate** — Small percentage on gifts received

## Strategic Fit with ljding.app Ecosystem
- **learn.ljding.app** — Could offer "wedding learning tracks" (financial planning for couples, cultural traditions courses)
- **hangwith.ljding.app** — Natural integration for wedding party coordination, bachelor/bachelorette planning
- **Privacy-first** — Wedding planning involves sensitive personal data (finances, family details) — aligns with ecosystem philosophy

## Feasibility Assessment
- **MVP Complexity**: Medium — guest list + RSVP + basic timeline is 6-8 weeks
- **Tech requirements**: Database for guests/vendors, email/SMS notifications, calendar integration
- **Differentiation**: High — bilingual web-first is genuine white space
- **Competitive moat**: Early bilingual content + vendor network
