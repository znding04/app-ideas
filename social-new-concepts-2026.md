### BodyDouble (同桌) — Camera-off Virtual Co-Working ⭐ RECOMMENDED #1

**Subdomain:** bodydouble.ljding.app  
**Chinese Name:** 同桌  
**Score: 19/20** (HIGHEST OF ALL APPS)

**Pitch:** Drop into a virtual co-working room with one other person — no chat, no video, just shared presence and a visible task timer that keeps you both honest.

**Core Features:**
- Instant 1:1 matching based on session length (25/50/90 min Pomodoro variants)
- Minimal UI: two task cards side-by-side with live countdown timers
- Optional ambient audio (cafe, rain, library hum) synced between partners
- End-of-session micro-reflection ("Did you finish? Rate your focus 1-5")
- Streak calendar showing co-working consistency over time

**Target User:** Remote workers and ADHD-affected adults who struggle with solo focus but find full video calls (Focusmate) too socially draining

**Differentiation:** Focusmate requires webcam-on video calls and scheduled bookings. 同桌 is camera-off, text-minimal, and instant-match — optimized for the "I just need someone silently there" use case. No accounts on the other person's end required; share a room link to co-work with a friend directly.

**Why #1 Build:** Smallest scope (2-3 weeks), highest score (19), complements Drift (focus sessions), and directly extends hangwith.ljding.app into virtual presence. Web-native PWA works on any device.

### Linjù (邻聚) — Hyper-Local Neighborhood Bulletin Board

**Subdomain:** linju.ljding.app  
**Chinese Name:** 邻聚  
**Score: 15/20**

**Pitch:** A hyper-local bulletin board for your building or block — borrow a ladder, find a dog-walker, organize a hallway potluck, all without joining yet another group chat.

**Core Features:**
- GPS-verified micro-zones (apartment building, block, or 500m radius)
- Post types: Lend/Borrow, Ask/Offer, Event, Lost & Found, Alert
- Ephemeral posts (auto-expire in 7 days) to keep the board fresh
- Anonymous posting option for sensitive asks (noise complaints, safety concerns)
- Karma score visible only to yourself — tracks helpfulness without gamifying reputation publicly

**Target User:** Urban apartment dwellers (especially new-to-city transplants and Chinese diaspora communities) who want neighborhood connection without Nextdoor's toxicity or WeChat group spam

**Differentiation:** Nextdoor is ad-heavy, identity-forward, and prone to surveillance culture. 邻聚 is ephemeral-first, privacy-respecting (no real names required), and scoped tighter — your building, not your zip code. Bilingual EN/ZH serves mixed-language neighborhoods that existing platforms ignore entirely.

### MentorRing (引路) — 4-Week Structured Micro-Mentorships

**Subdomain:** mentorring.ljding.app  
**Chinese Name:** 引路  
**Score: 17/20**

**Pitch:** Structured 4-week micro-mentorships between strangers — one specific skill, one clear goal, then graceful graduation.

**Core Features:**
- Skill-based matching: mentor lists what they can teach, mentee lists what they need (e.g., "mock interview prep," "first open-source PR," "negotiate a raise in EN")
- Built-in 4-week arc: Week 1 intro call, Weeks 2-3 async check-ins with shared doc, Week 4 wrap-up and mutual feedback
- Conversation prompts and templates for each week to reduce awkwardness
- Mutual opt-out at any checkpoint — no guilt, no penalty
- Public "graduated" badges that mentees can display (mentors stay anonymous unless they opt in)

**Target User:** Early-career professionals and international students who need guidance but lack access to formal mentorship programs; experienced professionals who want to give back in bounded, low-commitment ways

**Differentiation:** ADPList and MentorCruise are ongoing, open-ended relationships that create guilt around disengagement. LinkedIn mentorship is effectively dead. 引路 solves the "how does this end?" problem by designing the ending into the structure from day one. The time-boxed format lowers commitment anxiety on both sides.

### HēiHē (嘿合) — Interest Circles of Exactly 7 People

**Subdomain:** heihei.ljding.app  
**Chinese Name:** 嘿合  
**Score: 17/20**

**Pitch:** Interest-based micro-communities of exactly 7 people — small enough that everyone talks, big enough that it's not awkward if one person is quiet.

**Core Features:**
- Browse open "circles" by interest tag (e.g., #sourdough-beginner, #running-5k-training, #reading-scifi, #parenting-toddler)
- Each circle caps at 7 members; when full, a new one spawns
- Weekly themed prompt (auto-generated or member-submitted) to spark conversation
- Shared media board for photos/links relevant to the interest
- Circles auto-archive after 8 weeks of inactivity; members get a "memory capsule" export

**Target User:** People who crave genuine community around niche interests but find Discord servers overwhelming, Reddit impersonal, and Facebook Groups full of noise

**Differentiation:** Geneva and Discord optimize for large communities with roles and channels. 嘿合 enforces smallness as a feature — the fixed cap of 7 creates intimacy that scales horizontally (many small circles) rather than vertically (one big server). The auto-archive prevents zombie communities from cluttering the experience.

### QìngBǎn (庆伴) — Async Celebration Wall

**Subdomain:** qingban.ljding.app  
**Chinese Name:** 庆伴  
**Score: 17/20**

**Pitch:** An async celebration wall where friends pile on encouragement for someone's milestone — like a digital group card that actually feels warm.

**Core Features:**
- Create a "celebration board" for someone: birthday, promotion, new baby, marathon finish, graduation, or custom milestone
- Invite contributors via link — no account needed to leave a message, voice note, photo, or short video
- Board reveals to the recipient at a scheduled time (surprise mode) or stays open (rolling mode)
- Templates and prompts to help contributors who freeze on "what do I write?"
- Export as shareable image collage, PDF keepsake, or video montage

**Target User:** Friend groups, families, and coworkers (especially distributed/remote ones) who want to celebrate someone meaningfully but find Kudoboard generic and group chat "happy birthday!" threads underwhelming

**Differentiation:** Kudoboard is corporate and template-heavy. Group ecards feel impersonal. 庆伴 focuses on warmth over polish — voice notes and casual photos over stock graphics. The no-account-needed contribution flow eliminates the friction that kills most group gift/card platforms. Bilingual support makes it natural for families that span EN/ZH contexts.
